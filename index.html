Western Australia
Snorkelling

Let's go!

Western Australia is fortunate to be home to world class beaches, with extraordinary snorkelling spots all along the coast. The waters of Western Australia are filled with shipwrecks, corals, and marine life. Our beautiful Summer weather means that any day is the perfect day to pack your snorkel, bathers, and towel, and head down to the beach to do some snorkelling.

Let's Go Somewhere Amazing

Omeo Shipwreck

Located at the North end of Coogee Beach, Omeo Shipwreck is the perfect snorkelling spot for families, beginners, and anyone looking to dip their toe into a bit of snorkelling. With calm waters, ample parking, and easy public transport access, you're sure to have a great day at Omeo!

Plan Your Trip

What should I bring?

It's important to be prepared for your snorkelling trip, and that means bringing everything that you might need. You should always bring:

  • Sunscreen, sunnies, and a hat for protection against the Aussie sun.
  • Goggles or a snorkel — how else are you going to explore the waters?
  • Bathers and a towel
  • Your phone so you can stay contactable.
  • Plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • A beach bag to put everything in.

Where should I go?

Western Australia is home to dozens of beautiful snorkelling spots, with locations suitable to a wide range of skill levels. When deciding where to go, you should consider:

  • The ocean and swell conditions: weak swimmers or beginner snorkellers should always go snorkelling somewhere with calm waters.
  • Nearby facilities and amenities: you may want to wash the salt off you, or grab a bite to eat after your swim!
  • Transportation: some spots are easier to get to than others. If you're going by public transport, you may want to find somewhere closer to train or bus routes.
  • Popularity: there's strength in numbers, so you may want to go somewhere where you know there will be other swimmers around. Alternatively, you may want to go somewhere a bit more quiet, and have the reef to yourself.

What time is best?

Ocean conditions are always best in the morning. For pristine calm waters, you'll want to get to the beach between 7am and 10am. You can also check wind conditions in many weather apps, to stay extra informed.

For safety and better visibility, it is recommended that you only go snorkelling while the sun is up and there is plenty of brightness. If it's getting dark, you're better off waiting for some more sunshine.

Who should I go with?

There's always strength in numbers. Whether you're a beginner or a pro, you should always bring someone with you. Always look out for each other in the water, and let someone else know where you're going.

Is there anything else I should know?

Make sure you're well-prepared for your snorkelling trip. Know the conditions, bring plenty of water and sun protection, and make sure someone knows where you're going. Visit this page to learn more.

Stay safe out there

Going for a snorkelling trip is exciting, but you should always take care to stay safe. Before hopping in the water, make sure you're well aware of the ocean conditions, stay contactable, learn about any sealife dangers, and go with a buddy - there's safety in numbers! And, always let someone know where you're going. Taking these few easy steps will ensure a safe and enjoyable trip for everyone!

Coogee Beach
Omeo Wreck

A snorkeller's paradise

Located 30 minutes from Perth's CBD, Omeo Shipwreck is the perfect snorkelling location for beginners and families. There is ample parking for those getting there by car, as well as a nearby bus stop with hourly services. When you're done exploring the shipwreck, you can sunbake on the warm Coogee Beach sand, or head to a nearby cafe to refresh and refuel.

How to get here

By Public Transport

By Car

Omeo Wreck, Coogee Beach, and the surrounding suburb of Port Coogee, can all be easily accessed by the 512 bus route, which has hourly services from Murdoch Train Station.

  • Perth Underground Train Station
    Catch Mandurah line train, platform 2
  • Murdoch Train Station
    Transfer to bus 512, stand 4
  • Orsino Boulevard After Perlinte View Bus Stop
    Exit bus and walk to Omeo Wreck
  • Omeo Wreck
    Arrive

There's plenty of parking around Coogee Beach and Omeo Wreck. However, on weekends, as well as during the Summer holidays, parking can still be quite a challenge. We suggest arriving early, to beat the crowds and get a good parking spot.
